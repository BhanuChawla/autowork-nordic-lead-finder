Target Customer Profile

Independent Tyre Shops & Workshops Without E-Commerce

Independent tyre shops, auto workshops, and small-to-medium tyre retailers in the Nordics that currently sell tyres offline or have a basic web presence without e-commerce capability. Prime prospects are those offering tyre hotel (seasonal storage) services.

Tyre Retail Auto Workshop Tyre Wholesale Tyre Hotel / Storage Fast-Fit Centre

Key Pain Points to Listen For

  • Missing online sales — Losing customers to online retailers like Däckonline, Amazon, and chain competitors
  • Seasonal revenue management — Revenue dips between winter/summer tyre change seasons with no recurring online income
  • Manual tyre hotel tracking — Managing seasonal storage inventory with spreadsheets or paper
  • No booking system — Fitting appointments managed by phone only, missing walk-in and online bookings
  • No online price visibility — Customers asking for quotes when competitors show prices online instantly
  • EV tyre demand — Growing demand for specialised EV tyres (heavier, lower rolling resistance) they can't easily advertise

Buying Triggers to Watch For

  • Losing customers to online-only retailers and chains with e-commerce
  • Growing tyre hotel business that needs digital management
  • Seasonal capacity management challenges (booking peaks)
  • Desire to reach younger, digitally-native car owners
  • Expanding to multiple locations or workshop bays
  • Competitor nearby has launched online ordering

Nordic Market Context

  • Mandatory winter tyres — All Nordic countries require winter tyres, creating two annual peak seasons for tyre changes
  • Tyre hotel culture — Seasonal storage is extremely popular — many urban dwellers lack garage space. Key recurring revenue driver
  • EV growth — Nordics have the highest EV adoption in Europe. EVs need specialised tyres — a growth segment
  • Digital-first consumers — Nordic consumers are among the most digitally savvy in Europe with high e-commerce adoption
  • Market size — 10,000+ tyre-related businesses across the Nordics
